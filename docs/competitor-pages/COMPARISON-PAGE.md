### 2. TL;DR verdict box

- Who should pick CogniFit
- Who should pick the competitor
- Bottom line recommendation

### 3. Feature matrix (comparison table)
Required columns: feature · CogniFit · competitor(s). Required rows listed in template below.

### 8. Real user verdicts
3–5 short testimonials (existing reviews from CogniFit) + G2/Capterra/Trustpilot aggregate scores with source links. Include at least one "switched from [competitor]" story.

### Clinical validation deep dive (250 words minimum)

CogniFit's assessments are validated against gold-standard neuropsychological test batteries. The platform has been used in 1,083+ peer-reviewed clinical trials and research studies, including work at Harvard Medical School, University of Washington, and Tel Aviv University. The landmark ACTIVE study (Advanced Cognitive Training for Independent and Vital Elderly), published in the Journal of the American Geriatrics Society, demonstrated that structured cognitive training can deliver benefits lasting 5 to 10 years.

Lumosity's training framework (LPI — Lumosity Performance Index) has been studied, including the 2015 Human Cognition Project. However, in 2016 the FTC fined Lumos Labs $2 million for unsubstantiated claims that Lumosity prevents cognitive decline or Alzheimer's disease. The settlement required corrective advertising. Lumosity remains a respected consumer product but is positioned today as entertainment and mental workout, not clinical.

For users whose goal is cognitive longevity, dementia-risk reduction, or evidence-based training, CogniFit's peer-reviewed research base and FDA-recognized digital endpoints make it the stronger choice.

### ITS vs Lumosity's approach (150 words)

CogniFit uses a patented Individualized Training System (ITS) that adapts in real time to your cognitive profile. Each session adjusts difficulty, targets weakest areas first, and optimizes based on your last performance. The underlying engine has been refined over 25+ years of research.

Lumosity uses a progressive difficulty ladder with periodic recalibration. The experience feels polished and game-like, but the same exercises are delivered to users with very different cognitive profiles. For casual users this is fine. For anyone tracking measurable improvement in specific domains (working memory, executive function, divided attention), CogniFit's per-user ITS adaptation produces more targeted gains.
