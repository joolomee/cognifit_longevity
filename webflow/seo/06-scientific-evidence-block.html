Peer-Reviewed Evidence

Published research behind CogniFit

  1. Shah TM, Weinborn M, Verdile G, Sohrabi HR, Martins RN. Enhancing Cognitive Functioning in Healthy Older Adults: A Systematic Review of the Clinical Significance of Commercially Available Computerized Cognitive Training in Preventing Cognitive Decline. Neuropsychology Review, 2017; 27(1): 62–80.

    After reviewing approximately 8,000 studies, reviewers concluded that CogniFit possesses the highest level of evidence among commercial programs for healthy ageing.

  2. Peretz C, Korczyn AD, Shatil E, Aharonson V, Birnboim S, Giladi N. Computer-Based, Personalized Cognitive Training versus Classical Computer Games: A Randomized Double-Blind Prospective Trial of Cognitive Stimulation. Neuroepidemiology, 2011; 36(2): 91–99. DOI: 10.1159/000323950
  3. Shatil E, Mikulecká J, Bellotti F, Burěs V. Novel Television-Based Cognitive Training Improves Working Memory and Executive Function. PLOS ONE, 2014; 9(7): e101472. DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0101472
